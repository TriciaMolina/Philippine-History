History of the Philippines

The Philippines' history begins at least 709,000 years ago, with the earliest known hominid activity in the archipelago. An extinct human species known as Homo luzonensis lived on the island of Luzon at least 134,000 years ago. The Tabon Caves in Palawan are home to the earliest known anatomically modern human, which lived approximately 47,000 years ago. The earliest people to settle in the ancient Philippines were negrito clans.South Asians, Papuans, and Austronasiatics came next. The bulk of the existing population is made up of maritime Austronesians who migrated southward from Taiwan around approximately 3000 BCE. The archipelago served as the center of a transoceanic Philippine jade culture by the year 2000 BCE.
